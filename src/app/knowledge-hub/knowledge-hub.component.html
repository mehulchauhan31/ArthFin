    Understanding Investor Behavior
    Remember, adopting smart investor behavior results in positive investments that contribute to growing your wealth.
    But what exactly is smart investor behavior?
    To understand investor behavior is, you need to be familiar with the DALBAR Study conducted in USA . The original DALBAR Study found that the average return from the top 500 U.S. listed companies between 1980 to 2000 in the U.S.A was 12%. In contrast, the average return for investors over the same period was 4%.

    So, what was the cause of that 8% difference ?

    Three words : Bad investor behavior.

    Unsound investor behavior can lead to adverse investment outcomes, potentially hindering the growth of your wealth.

    The DALBAR Study found that the average holding period by investors who claimed they were investing for the long term was just over 3 years. It was found that market decline caused these investors to panic and sell at the wrong time based upon world events happening g at the time that was out of their control. The result: consistently lower returns than those investors who were patient and planned for the true long-term.
